Project Overview

Designed, constructed, and experimentally validated a three-band analog audio equalizer with independent bass, midrange, and treble control. The system integrates passive RC filters, op-amp gain stages, signal recombination, and a power amplifier capable of driving an 8 Ω speaker while meeting strict frequency, ripple, and power specifications.

Full test cases, supplement files, and schematics are included in the report below and on GitHub.

Technical Stack

  • Analog circuit design (RC filters, op-amp amplification)
  • Operational amplifiers (LM324, OP07)
  • LM386 audio power amplifier
  • LTSpice simulation and hardware prototyping
  • Oscilloscope-based frequency response and RMS measurements

Engineering Approach

  • Designed low-pass, band-pass, and high-pass RC filters to isolate bass (<320 Hz), midrange (320–3200 Hz), and treble (>3200 Hz) frequency bands within ±10% tolerance.
  • Implemented buffered, inverting op-amp gain stages with potentiometer control to allow independent amplification or attenuation of each frequency band.
  • Recombined filtered signals using a summing amplifier and validated low ripple performance, then integrated a power amplifier stage capable of delivering >400 mW to an 8 Ω load across the audio frequency range.

Key Takeaways

  • Strengthened understanding of analog filter design, cutoff frequency selection, and the impact of component tolerances on real-world performance.
  • Gained hands-on experience designing stable op-amp gain stages, summing amplifiers, and managing signal integrity across multiple analog stages.
  • Learned to experimentally validate full-system performance using frequency response, ripple analysis, and power measurements to align with simulations.
