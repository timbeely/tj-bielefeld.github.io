Project Overview

Developed and tested a PWM LED using a 555-Timer integrated circuit. This circuit uses a potentiometer and a modified astable 555-timer configuration to control the brightness of LEDs. Also tested configuations of monostable and astable modes to ensure proper behavior of the IC.

Full LTSpice schematics and simulations are included in the report below.

Technical Stack

  • LTSpice
  • 555 Timer IC (NE555)
  • Analog circuit design and breadboarding
  • Pulse Width Modulation (PWM)
  • Oscilloscope, Multimeter, Waveform Generator

Engineering Approach

  • Designed and implemented a PWM LED dimmer using a modified astable 555-timer configuration with a potentiometer to control duty cycle.
  • Analyzed monostable and astable operating modes to verify correct timing behavior and understand internal comparator and discharge transistor operation.
  • Validated circuit performance through voltage, frequency, and duty-cycle measurements, ensuring consistent LED brightness control.

Key Takeaways

  • Developed a strong understanding of PWM as an efficient method for analog brightness control using digital-style signals.
  • Gained hands-on experience analyzing timing equations, duty-cycle control, and component tolerances in analog circuits.
  • Improved confidence troubleshooting real hardware using test equipment and translating theory into practical circuit behavior.
